I'll wait until Jacob gets here, he carries the Commandments on his jacket. He's the Commandment Bearer, if anyone tries to take him down, we gotta kill him first, it's kind of cool. We haven't had to do that yet, because nobody cares, and I'm glad nobody cares, because I don't want anybody to care. The worst thing that we ever had happen to us was when the fucking Hells Angels tried to... I was in this bar called Whiskey Junction one night, and this guy came up to us, he's one of those guys who hangs out with biker gangs and really wants to get, you know, he sucks everyone dick or something like that. I don't know his name, I wish I did, because if you're going to write this in a major paper I'd like to tell him a little message. Which I'll tell him another day, anyway. But anyway, so, he says, ``Man, you can't wear those fuckin' colors'', and I'm like, ``what are you talking about?'' He's like, ``Those are Hell's Angel's colors.'' And I was like, ``Okay, well I'll talk to the President of the Hells Angels some other day. He's like, ``Well, he's sitting right over there!'' So I'm like, okay, I guess I have to go talk to him now, because if I don't, this guy's gonna go over there and start a bunch of shit, and we're gonna get our kneecaps broken, and tire tracks on our fucking heads. Wide tire tracks. I fuckin' hate wide tire tracks. Anyway, so I walk over to him, and say, ``I hear there was some controversy.'' I was really courteous about it and everything. I said, ``I heard there was some controversy, I heard that there's a conflict between the colors that I wear and the colors that you wear.'' He said, ``Where are they? Let me see them.'' I say, ``They're in my van right now.'' So I take him out to my van, which is this huge black van, it's real cheesy, it looks like a gangster van, it's got no windows and shit. It's like the A-team's. I bring him up there, and he's fixing his pistol and putting on his lead-plated glove and shit. I turn around and I'm like, ``Dude, I'm not fucking with you. You could destroy me about that quick, no matter what.'' He's got his bodyguard with him, who would bump that ledge up there. Anyway, so, he looks at them and says, ``I have to bring these to the clubhouse and talk to the rest of the boys.'' So I'm like, What-ever, man, you're a fucking bore. If I was doing this during `toys for tots', you'd be too busy to fucking care, cause you'd be running drugs in teddy bears and shit like that. Anyway, we go back into the club, and they're all laughing at us, and shit, they're like, ``Oh, you're a bicycle club!'', and I'm like, ``Yeah, didn't you notice the weird bicycles out here and shit?'' They were laughing. Finally, we went to the clubhouse the next day, and they were like, ``We're gonna have to ceremoniously burn these jackets.'' I'm like, ``Okay, whatever. That's cool, have fun. I hope you have a raving good time.'' What the fuck, you know? So, I don't know why I started talking about this. That was like, we were on the topic of the stupidest thing that ever happened?
Megulon-5. About people giving a shit.
A. I can't believe that we threatened a group of people who were the first biker gang in practically the fuckin' world almost, except for old ones like the Wild Angels, little ones, the Devil's Prophets. Like the Hell's Angels, they're the major largest biker club in the world. The guys in Russia, the Hell's Angels, the main chapter in Russia, in the Soviet Union, they have one clubhouse, the size of this fucking room that we're in, and they have a Harley Davidson that is not even working. I saw a picture of them in, what's that biker magazine, they were like, ``Our motorcycle is not working yet. But soon we shall receive the parts, as soon as our country is free.'' It's these 50 guys, and they take turns sitting on the motorcycle, like they're riding it, and get pictures taken with these big Helga women sitting on the back. Really super-funny. It's like, god, man, I'm just doing it for fun.
M. ``You Just Want To Ride Your Bike.''
A. Fuck, I'm just having fun.

I was like, ``Oh my god, man, I can't believe that you're so fucking dumb.'' That night we were doing an interview in the Reader. That night that was what we just finished doing. So I was like, ``Okay, that's cool, now we got an interview in the goddamn paper. And they're gonna read that, and we're gonna be out of town [touring with the Cirkus]. There's a guy named Chester that we know. He never gave up his colors. He's like, ``Fuck you. I'm not taking off my colors for nothing.'' These guys attacked him. He's just that way. So I can see that if anyone came up and fucked with him that he would just confuse them completely. Not intentionally, but that's just how he is.
A. This whole thing started when I was a kid I saw a tall bike, they've been made since the 40's, as far as I know, that's the only history I know.
M. I ran into this old guy who said, ``Yeah, we use to ride those things all the time!''

A. The Golden Cow.

A. Yeah, it is the icon of the whole thing. It's like that fucking longest-ass chopper in the world, which I'll never touch, thank you, because it's too long, and it's too heavy, and it's annoying. The thing is so fucking unstable that... But then Jacob broke it, we ride it into our friend Per's shop, and that was the beginning. That was about four and a half years ago. We just started pumping them out and everyone started riding them for the fun of it, and we made these patches, and we were having fun, and we were going to the motorcycle rallies, and showing them that there's more than just motorcycles and two wheels.

What's shakin'?'' I turn away like I'm not really me, I'm like ``oh, la la lalala,'', and I look back, and he's gone. And then I'll go down to the West Bank, I'm driving on the fucking freeway, and he beats me. Walking. He doesn't have a car, the guy doesn't own anything. He doesn't have a pot to piss in. He owns a fucking pair of pants that he pisses in, you know? That's one of the phenomenas of traveling. That's what I'm into. I want him to give me some clues before he, you know, so I can pass them on.
