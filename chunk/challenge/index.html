The Wheeled Ginormity Challenge.

Occasionally in our travels we encounter vehicles which are billed as the World's Tallest Tallbike or the World's Longest Chopper or whatever. They've always been impressive bikes, and possibly even worthy of a world record, but really. World exposure is needed before claims of world domination can be asserted.

We ourselves are not as interested in finding the single tallest or longest or most puzzling bicycle that is to be found on the planet. There are already organizations, such as the Guinness Book of World Records, that are looked toward to provide these factoids. We don't necessarily agree or disagree with their decisions, either - we only appreciate bikes that hit the streets and interact with people, while Guinness is satisfied with a museum exhibit that has wobbled fearfully around a parking lot once. The tallest unicycles are taller than the tallest tallbikes, anyway.

Instead, we wish to honor those who strive to create bikes that are among the longest, tallest, and mightiest. None of us on this earth today can compare our rides to the tallbikes of the Nephilim, yet even they were not able to keep their heads above the waters!

We therefore provide this space to allow the pedal-powered monstrosity engineers of the planet to formally stake their claims to enter the ranks of the Tallest Tallbikes and Longest Choppers. Present your images and any supporting documentation to us via the address below, and we will present them here.

Prizes.

Nothing worth squabbling about. Winners will receive glory and a night on the town with C.H.U.N.K. 666 during which a trophy or a medal or something will be presented, if we happen to be in the same town while the record is still held and the bike is still ridable. This is an ongoing competition.

Rules.

  • The height of a tallbike will be measured from the saddle to the ground. The length of a chopper will be measured from the front wheel to the back wheel.
  • For Longest Chopper entries, the distance between the head tube and the front wheel must be at least as long as the distance between the head tube and the rear wheel. If there is more than one head tube, the one closest to the front wheel will be used for this measurement.
  • Entries must be solely human powered.
  • Entries must be rideable. The bike must be ridden on a loop course such as a city block or equivalent, where the starting point is the same as the ending point, during which time only the wheels may touch the ground or other objects - no dabbing, poling, or similar. The rider must start from a complete stop on level ground, with no human assistance such as holding up or pushing, although leaning against a building or other object and pushing off at the starting line is permissible.
  • Only two wheels may touch the ground or any other object while the course is being navigated - no trikes, outriggers, wheelie bars, etc. The vehicle may have more than two wheels, but any wheels exceeding two must not touch the ground during this time. Single-track vehicles only, no steamrollers or other crafty cheats.
  • Entries must be ridden at least occasionally outside of the competition. It doesn't matter where or how - parades, milk runs, lamplighting duties, whatever - but the bike must be taken out and ridden once in a while.
  • These rules may be amended and modified at any time, as there's always something that wasn't thought of for this kind of thing. We will show no favoritism, and in return ask contestants to be sporting.

To Enter.

Send images, measurements, and corroborating evidence to <megulon5@dclxvi.org> or to C.H.U.N.K. 666, POB 5791, Portland, OR 97228. You must give us permission to reprint these here and in print publications, and you must give us a means of contacting you, although you may ask to remain anonymous. Entries must include an image of the vehicle being ridden, a description of the vehicle and how it came to be built, a description of the course listed below, and an account, whether exciting or boring, of riding the vehicle in an activity unrelated to this contest.
